Foundations

Design principles

These principles are about interface quality, not campaign language. They should be visible in layout, typography, component states, motion, and screen architecture.

01

Native clarity

Screens should feel close to the operating system: direct, readable, immediate, and free of decorative friction.

02

Data before decoration

Amounts, score, due dates, eligibility, and state labels are always more important than visual effects.

03

Glass with purpose

Use glass to separate layers and focus attention. Do not place long text, tables, or legal content on weak transparent surfaces.

04

Quiet confidence

Prefer restraint: fewer colors, larger spacing, exact labels, controlled contrast, and calm confirmations.

05

Motion as feedback

Animation should reveal structure, confirm actions, and express intelligence without changing layout width or distracting from decisions.

06

Accessible luxury

Premium design must still be readable, keyboard reachable, touch safe, and clear in light mode, dark mode, and reduced motion.

Personality

Visual attributes

The brand should not become flashy or overly playful. The right visual attitude is technical, clean, luminous, and warm only at controlled accent points.

Luminous

Soft cyan light, clear surfaces, and subtle highlights that suggest speed and precision.

Measured

Gold appears in small amounts for value, achievement, and premium selection states.

Exact

Numbers align, states are explicit, and controls maintain stable dimensions.

Calm

Whitespace, predictable grids, restrained shadows, and no constantly moving calls to action.

Logo system

Mark usage standards

The mark is a trust anchor. Use it cleanly and consistently. It should never compete with hero type, app data, or primary actions.

Clear space

Keep at least one symbol-width around the logo. In dense navigation, the icon can stand alone if the product name is nearby.

Minimum size

App icon in header: 32px minimum. Website navigation: 40px. Brand display: 96px or larger. Never render the mark blurry.

Backgrounds

Preferred backgrounds are white glass, cyan gradient, deep navy, or screenshot surfaces with a clear scrim behind the mark.

Clearspace model

Incorrect usage

  • xDo not stretch the symbol or force it into wide containers.
  • xDo not recolor the white mark with random gradients or unapproved tints.
  • xDo not rotate, tilt, outline, emboss, or add heavy drop shadows.
  • xDo not crowd the mark with badges, status chips, or promotional labels.

Color system

Functional color roles

Color is a UI system, not decoration. Cyan handles brand presence and active states. Navy creates trust and contrast. Gold is rare and premium. Rose is only a small warmth accent.

Gradient rule

Use at most two dominant brand colors with one small gold highlight. Avoid rainbow effects.

Dark mode

Use navy instead of pure black. Reduce white opacity, increase border contrast, preserve cyan glow.

Layout

Grid, spacing, and responsive rules

Layout should make the interface feel organized before any visual effect is added. Use stable dimensions and responsive rules for mobile, tablet, and desktop.

Desktop web

Container: 1120-1180px. Use 12 columns, 24px gutters, and compact hero height.

Tablet

Use 24-32px gutters, 2-column content, fewer stacked cards, and tighter vertical rhythm.

Mobile

Use 16px gutters, 8px micro spacing, 12-16px component gaps, and 44px touch targets.

Liquid glass material

Surface standards

Glass is the signature material. It should create depth, separation, and focus without hurting readability. Use stronger surfaces for dense data and weaker surfaces for decorative background layers.

Light glass

White transparency, soft border, inner highlight, subtle shadow, and readable dark text.

Dark glass

Lower white opacity, stronger border, subtle cyan glow, and no pure black surfaces.

Blur

18px for compact controls, 22px for cards, 28px for navigation or hero glass.

Fill opacity

Light: 30-58 percent white. Dark: 6-14 percent white with stronger border.

Border

1px alpha border with a brighter top edge. Increase contrast over imagery.

Shadow

Large, soft, low-opacity shadow. Never use hard black outlines or heavy glow.

Use glass for
  • Navigation bars and bottom sheets
  • Primary data cards and score panels
  • Focused controls, modals, and overlays
  • Hero screenshot framing
Avoid glass for
  • Long legal text or dense tables
  • Low-contrast copy over busy images
  • Every single component on a screen
  • Animated backgrounds that change page width

Mobile app UX

Screen architecture standards

Mobile UI should answer: where am I, what is my current state, what can I do next, and what happens if I continue. Use the screenshots as visual references for density, color, and top-cropped phone framing.

Mobile hierarchy model

  • 1Status layer: greeting, profile, notification, security, and current account context.
  • 2Primary value: available limit, active balance, score, repayment amount, or application state.
  • 3Action layer: one primary action, secondary tools, and clear route to detail.
  • 4Evidence layer: score factors, terms, fees, due dates, progress, and explanations.
  • 5Navigation layer: stable bottom nav with selected state and safe-area padding.

Motion

Animation standards

Motion should clarify hierarchy, make state changes understandable, and create a premium sense of responsiveness. It must never create horizontal overflow or make CTAs move constantly.

Reveal

Fade, 8-30px translate, 10-16px blur, 700-1000ms. Trigger on load and scroll.

Signal

Subtle glow, orbit, shimmer, or count-up only for intelligence and score modules.

Interaction

Buttons and controls respond with color, shadow, or scale below 1.02. No layout shift.

Reduced motion

Respect system preferences. Keep opacity changes and remove decorative movement.

Motion type Timing Easing Use
Micro interaction 160-220ms ease-out Button hover, chip select, toggle change.
Screen reveal 700-1000ms cubic-bezier(.2,.8,.2,1) Hero, sections, cards, phone mockups.
Count-up 900-1400ms ease-out Score, limit, portfolio metric after entering view.
Ambient signal 4-9s loop linear or ease-in-out Background shine only. Keep behind content and inside overflow-hidden bounds.

Imagery

Screenshot and asset standards

Product visuals should reveal real interface detail. Avoid generic stock images, excessive blur, dark unreadable crops, and mockups that hide the actual UI.

Phone framing

Use top-centered crops. Show the status bar, first value, and first action. Avoid extreme tilt.

Light / dark pairing

When a page supports theme switching, use the matching screenshot. Do not show dark UI in light mode.

Background media

Use cyan-blue surfaces, real app screenshots, or generated abstract light fields with enough contrast.

Accessibility

Quality and usability standards

Premium UI is measured by clarity under pressure: readable terms, predictable controls, visible state changes, and clear recovery when something fails.

Contrast

Body text must remain readable over glass. Add scrims or stronger fills over imagery.

Touch

Minimum 44px targets. Keep destructive actions away from primary confirmation controls.

Focus

Keyboard focus must be visible on links, buttons, fields, segmented controls, and cards.

Financial clarity

Fees, repayment schedules, consent, and score explanations must be visible before confirmation.

Language

Use short labels, sentence case, plain error messages, and direct next actions.

Resilience

Every flow needs loading, empty, offline, pending, success, warning, and error states.

Design QA

Release checklist

Check every page, screen, and component against this list before delivery.

  • Text does not overlap at 320px, 390px, tablet, and desktop widths.
  • No animated element changes document width or creates horizontal scroll.
  • Light and dark modes both use the correct screenshot and contrast.
  • Primary action is visually clear and does not move constantly.
  • Financial values use tabular numerals when they animate or compare.
  • Glass surfaces have enough border and fill contrast against their background.
  • Icons match a consistent stroke style and do not replace critical labels.
  • Loading, empty, success, warning, error, offline, and pending states are designed.
  • Reduced motion mode remains usable and polished.
  • Every financial decision screen explains the next action before commitment.
  • Tablet layouts are reviewed separately, not treated as stretched desktop.
  • Legal, fee, repayment, and consent text stays readable over non-glass surfaces.
